# ADR-012: Knowledge-to-Content Generation Policy

## Status

Proposed

## Date

2026-06-10

## Context

The Apple LLM Wiki is not only a storage layer. Future users may want to generate question banks, short video scripts, retail training scripts, and FABE sales scripts from wiki knowledge.

These outputs are application-layer content. They must be generated from facts, evidence, and curated wiki pages without weakening the trust model. The system must prevent unsupported marketing claims, stale recommendations, and content that cannot be traced back to source-backed knowledge.

## Decision

Create a governed content generation layer on top of the wiki.

Generated content must be traceable to source-backed facts when it makes factual claims. Recommendations, teaching structure, and sales framing may be generated by the LLM, but they must remain clearly separated from facts.

## Supported Output Types

Initial supported content types:

- question bank items
- short video scripts
- retail demonstration scripts
- FABE scripts
- training summaries

Each output type should have a schema, citation rules, freshness rules, and review level.

## Generation Pipeline

```text
content request
→ intent and audience definition
→ entity and fact retrieval
→ freshness and citation check
→ output schema selection
→ draft generation
→ validation
→ review or publish
```

The LLM should not generate directly from raw source documents unless the content is explicitly marked as draft and unreviewed.

## Question Bank Generation

Question items should include:

- question type
- difficulty
- topic entity
- prompt
- choices, if applicable
- correct answer
- explanation
- source references
- freshness status

Example:

```yaml
type: single_choice
difficulty: basic
topic: product:iphone-15-pro
prompt: Which chip does iPhone 15 Pro use?
choices:
  - A16 Bionic
  - A17 Pro
  - M1
  - H2
answer: A17 Pro
explanation: iPhone 15 Pro uses the A17 Pro chip.
source_refs:
  - source:apple-tech-specs-iphone-15-pro
```

Question generation must avoid stale facts unless the question is historical.

## Short Video Script Generation

Short video scripts should include:

- platform
- target length
- audience
- hook
- narration
- visual cues
- key facts
- call to action
- source references for factual claims

Claims such as performance, battery life, compatibility, and pricing must be supported by facts.

## Retail Demonstration Scripts

Retail scripts should focus on practical explanation and customer context.

Required fields:

- customer scenario
- product entity
- key talking points
- demonstration steps
- likely customer questions
- evidence-backed claims
- disclaimers for stale or region-specific information

## FABE Scripts

FABE means:

- Feature
- Advantage
- Benefit
- Evidence

FABE scripts are allowed when evidence is explicit.

Example:

```yaml
feature: A17 Pro chip
advantage: Higher graphics and compute capability
benefit: Better experience for gaming, video editing, and Pro workflows
evidence:
  - fact:iphone-15-pro:uses-chip
  - source:apple-tech-specs-iphone-15-pro
```

The `Evidence` section must cite facts or sources. Unsupported benefits must be rewritten as cautious guidance or removed.

## Review Levels

Allowed review levels:

- `auto_publish`: low-risk educational content from historical facts
- `needs_review`: sales scripts, recommendations, current product advice
- `blocked`: content with missing evidence, stale facts, or unsupported claims

Buying advice, current pricing, availability, and sales scripts should usually require review.

## Validation Rules

Generated content must pass:

- schema validation
- citation validation
- freshness validation
- unsupported claim detection
- locale check
- audience appropriateness check

## Consequences

Benefits:

- The wiki can power practical training and marketing workflows.
- Generated content remains traceable to facts.
- Sales and education outputs can share the same trusted knowledge base.
- FABE scripts become evidence-backed instead of purely rhetorical.

Costs:

- Requires output schemas and validators.
- Some generated content needs review.
- Content generation must track freshness and locale.

## Alternatives Considered

### Let Users Prompt Directly Against the Wiki

Rejected because direct prompting can produce unsupported, stale, or uncited content.

### Treat Generated Content as Facts

Rejected because generated scripts are outputs, not source-backed facts. They may contain reasoning and framing that should not enter the fact layer.

## Follow-up Work

- Define schemas for question bank, video script, retail script, and FABE output.
- Define unsupported claim detection rules.
- Define review workflow for generated content.
- Define export formats for training and retail use.
